Domestic Waste Management

Introduction

Domestic waste is a serious problem in all agglomerations of Mozambique due to a growth in population numbers and nonbiodegradable waste and the absence of sealed dumpsites.

  • Classification of solid waste components

  • Improper disposal of plastic waste from industrial processes can lead to land pollution. Plastic takes a long time to degrade, and accumulation in landfills is a significant concern.

  • Water Contamination:
    • Plastic waste can enter water bodies, affecting aquatic ecosystems. It may leach harmful chemicals, leading to water contamination.

  • Marine Pollution:
    • Improper disposal and inadequate waste management can result in plastic entering oceans, causing marine pollution. This poses threats to marine life through ingestion, entanglement, and habitat disruption.
  • Soil Degradation:
    • Plastic waste, when not properly managed, can degrade soil quality, affecting agricultural productivity and ecosystem health.
  • Resource Depletion:
    • The production of plastics relies on fossil fuels, contributing to resource depletion and environmental degradation associated with extraction and processing.
  • Ecosystem Disruption:
    • Plastic pollution can disrupt ecosystems by altering natural processes, impacting the balance of flora and fauna.
  • Microplastics:
    • Over time, larger plastic items can break down into smaller particles known as microplastics, which can have widespread and long-term ecological impacts.
  • Air Pollution:
    • Incineration of plastic waste in some industrial processes can release harmful pollutants into the air, contributing to air pollution and potential health hazards.

    preventive Measures

  • Reduce and Reuse: Encourage industries to minimize the use of single-use plastics and adopt reusable and sustainable alternatives.
  • Recycling:
    • Implement and promote effective recycling programs for industrial plastic waste to reduce the amount of plastic entering landfills and ecosystems.
  • Circular Economy :
    • Embrace a circular economy approach, where the lifecycle of plastic products is optimized through recycling, reusing, and reducing waste.
  • Regulations and Policies:
    • Implement and enforce strict regulations on the disposal and management of industrial plastic waste to ensure responsible practices.
  • Innovation and Research:
    • Invest in research and innovation to develop eco-friendly alternatives to traditional plastics and more sustainable industrial processes.

    Single-Use Plastics: Industries often use plastic for packaging, leading to a large amount of single-use plastic waste. This contributes significantly to the plastic pollution problem.

  • Manufacturing Waste:

  • Complexity of Plastic Recycling:
    • Many plastics are challenging to recycle due to variations in composition, color, and the presence of additives.
  • Limited Recycling Infrastructure:
    • Inadequate recycling infrastructure in many regions hinders the efficient collection and processing of plastic waste.
  • Pollution and Environmental Impact:
    • Land and Water Pollution: Improper disposal of plastic waste leads to pollution of land and water bodies. Plastics can take hundreds of years to degrade, causing long-term environmental damage.
  • Microplastics:
    • Over time, larger plastic items break down into smaller particles known as microplastics, which can enter ecosystems and potentially harm aquatic life.
  • Health Concerns:
  • Toxic Additives:
    • Some plastics contain additives that can be harmful to human health. When plastic waste degrades or incinerates, these additives may release toxins into the environment.
